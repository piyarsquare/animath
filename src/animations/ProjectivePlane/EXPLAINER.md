# Projective Plane

The **real projective plane RP²**, walked in first person. RP² is the sphere
with **opposite (antipodal) points glued together**: north pole = south pole,
and every point is the same place as the point straight through the centre.

## Why it's a sphere, not a flat tile

Unlike the torus and the Klein bottle, RP² **cannot be made flat** — by
Gauss–Bonnet its total curvature must equal 2π, so it is intrinsically curved.
The honest model is its **double cover, the sphere**: you walk on an ordinary
little planet, and locally it feels exactly like standing on a curved ground —
nothing strange happens underfoot, no teleport.

## Seeing the gluing

Because opposite points are identified, **everything appears twice** — once at a
point and once at its antipode on the far side. We draw that antipodal copy as a
**point reflection through the centre**, which is **orientation-reversing**. So:

- Walk a **great circle** and after a half-turn you reach the antipode — which
  *is* your starting point in RP². You arrive to find your own start, **mirror
  reversed**.
- Your **footprint arrows** (and the numbered pillars) come back with their
  left/right colors **swapped** on the far side. That mirror is what makes RP²
  **non-orientable**: there is no consistent global sense of "clockwise".

Drop footprints and walk around the planet to watch your trail meet its
reflected twin.
