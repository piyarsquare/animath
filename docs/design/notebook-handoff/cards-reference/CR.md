## note

`p` looks like a line, `−1 … 0 … +1`. It's secretly a circle. Swapping the
coordinates is `p ↦ 1/p`; push `p` outward and a rotation flattens into a shear —
the dual sits at both `0` and `∞`.

The three planes are one loop, not three boxes. [[QD]]

## full

`x ↔ y` sends `p ↦ 1/p`, fixing `±1` and swapping `0 ↔ ∞`. The complex arc (`p<0`)
and split arc (`p>0`) join at the two dual points; a rotation degenerating to a
shear is the loop passing through `p = 0`. [[QD]]
