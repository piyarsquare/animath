# The garden plan — beds, paths, and seams

> [!NOTE]
> Dan's brief (2026-07-03): the notebook should carry the *rhythm* of the last
> session — questions opening new ways of seeing, with the visualizations and
> tools provided to do the seeing. Analogy: **a garden**. We design the beds and
> the layouts; there are many paths, but they are natural to the layout — the
> visitor is not walking into the plantings. This plan reviews everything grown
> so far (35 cards, the app, the trail page, and the ideas that predate the
> recorded history) and proposes the main paths, what the plantings are, and
> where the seams show.

## The rhythm we are preserving

The 2026-06-29 session had a repeating unit, visible in its timeline:

> **a question → a reframe (a new way of seeing) → an instrument to see it with.**

Every card born that evening carries all three: *"is the fan preserved?"* →
rails are ideals → the Rays feed; *"what is this dial, really?"* → the knife's
tilt is the dial → the cone view; *"what happens to one value as p runs?"* →
the family self-describes → the proposed p-trace overlay; *"why does the
middle always stick?"* → sign-of-a-rescalable-invariant → the dial shows it.

This unit is the **walk step** of the notebook: each stop on a path poses a
question, turns it into a new way of seeing, and hands the visitor the
instrument. The stops are already written — they are the cards. What was
missing is the layout: which stops line up into which walks.

## Backward projection — the germ line

Ideas that predate the recorded unfolding, projected back so the paths honor
their real roots:

- **Arithmetic as geometry** (the Argand app, and behind it Plane
  Transform / Complex Particles): *add slides, multiply spirals* — an
  operation is a motion; a function is a place you move through. The site's
  founding instinct, and the reason every bed here has an instrument.
- **The first dial**: Argand's System slider `p = j²` — the family was a
  *slider* before it was a theory. The affine map as the complex cousin of
  `y = mx + b` is the oldest planting in the garden.
- **"Separable vs entangled" as the felt goal** and **the `t² = p` spine**
  (the 2026-06-24 plan): one count — roots, rails, eigenvalues, orbit shape —
  "one idea, four costumes." The change-of-basis morph was called "probably
  the single most important interaction" *before* it was built (it now exists
  as the app's rails slider).
- **The circling** (Dan, June 24): *"I circle these ideas from different
  perspectives"* — the same trichotomy deliberately re-met lens after lens.
  The garden must honor recurrence: paths that re-cross the same clearing
  from new directions are a feature, not a redundancy.
- **Web vs. single thread** — the one recorded reversal. June 24 planned an
  explorable *web*; June 29 chose a *single thread + post-marks*. **The garden
  is the synthesis**: several designed paths (not one, not arbitrary many),
  junctions where they cross, and beds the visitor never walks into. Curated
  and threaded, but not forced.

## The plantings — four beds, two trails, a wall of gates

Every one of the 35 cards places naturally into this layout:

**Bed 1 — The Line (the forcing bed).** Where the demands are named and shown
to leave *no* choices. Plantings: `L1 L3 L4 AX` + the facet `L5`
(*stretchable*, a groundcover that reappears in other beds). Instrument:
the a+b arrowhead widget (built — trail page 1 is this bed's walk, already
laid). Trailhead: **tropical** (the planted detour: give up subtraction).
Gates in the wall behind it: `fields · algebra · modular · p-adic`.

**Bed 2 — The Plaza (the choice).** The garden's center, where the one knob
lives. Plantings: `PL` (one knob), `DV` (three cuts), `L2` (the strangers'

the `j²` dial (built, in the app), the 3-way comparator (built — the app *is*
it), the jsq-dial widget (proposed on cards). Every path crosses the plaza.

**Bed 3 — The Three Parterres (the worlds).** Three formal beds around a
shared court, one per plane, deliberately parallel in layout so difference is
visible as difference: `CX` (+gates `matrices`, `analysis`), `DU` (+gate
`autodiff`), `SP` (+trail **relativity**, and the seam back to `L2`). The

the three plots, feeds (Point/Shape/Grid/Rays), Play, Iterate

**Bed 4 — The Overlook (the terrace).** The bed that *grew itself* in the

## The three main paths

Three interlocking walks, each with its own question-rhythm. They share
ground — that is the point — but each has its own direction of travel.

### Path A — "Could it be different?" (the forcing walk)

*Entrance → Bed 1 → the Plaza → the court's claims → the far gate.*

`L1 → AX → L3 → L4 —(tropical trailhead)→ PL → DV → FD → higher ⇢ cayley-dickson`

The Korzybski path — the notebook's thesis walk. Question rhythm: *is
(−)(−) = + a fact of nature?* → no, a theorem — so what forces it? → *what
survives the trip to the plane?* → one free number → *what if we demand
division everywhere?* → the dial is forced to −1 → *can we go higher?* → 1,
2, 4, 8, and it stops. The walk starts with zero choices, finds exactly one,
and ends where choice runs out again. Trail page 1 already lays this path's
first stretch; the rest is unlaid.

### Path B — "What does each world feel like?" (the residents' walk)

*The Plaza → the three parterres, in parallel → the court → the trails out.*

`C2/PL → DV → CX ‖ DU ‖ SP → WH → FD → (iterate: spiral · shear · saddle) → relativity / autodiff / analysis`

The experiential path — and it is **already walkable**: the Number Plane app
is this path built as an instrument. Question rhythm: *what does multiplying
DO here?* → turn / shear / squeeze → *is there a direction it can't turn?* →
count the rails → *what happens when you iterate?* → spiral, creep, snap →
*who lives in each world?* → analysis, autodiff, spacetime. Its
signature move is parallelism: the same expression planted in three beds at
once, difference read across the parterre.

### Path C — "It was one thing all along" (the overlook climb)

*Any bed → the terrace steps → the summit → the vista.*

`CN → CR → PT → NH → IN → CK`

The unification path — the last session's evening, laid as stone. Question
rhythm: *wait — ellipse/parabola/hyperbola is the same trichotomy?* → the
knife's tilt IS the dial (`p = a² − 1`) → *is the dial a line?* → no, a
circle; dual at 0 and ∞ → *fix a point, turn the dial — what does one value
do?* → a tame trace; the family differentiated at its own middle speaks
dual → *why does the middle always stick?* → sign of a rescalable invariant;
moduli = {−, 0, +} with a sticky wall → *is every trichotomy here the same
theorem?* → Sylvester's inertia, yes → *and beyond the wall?* → a second
axis, κ; nine geometries; our whole garden is one row of a bigger one.

This path's job is re-seeing: it must physically overlook the other beds
(each terrace stop should show the earlier instrument it reinterprets — the
cone view showing the dial it explains, the p-trace crossing the three plots
it samples).

**How they interlock:** A explains why the plaza exists; B lives in what the
plaza chooses; C reveals that A and B were about one object. A visitor can
walk any one alone; the design bet is that walking a second one after the
first is where the garden pays — the recurrence Dan named as "circling."

## The seams — where paths cross, deliberately visible

Dan asked that the stitching *reveal* the seams. These are the nine, each an
existing card edge; the notebook should render them as junctions (real
places), not links:

1. **`L2` = `SP`** *(same-as)* — the naive rival multiplication IS the split
   plane in disguise. Seam A×B: the forcing walk's counterexample turns out
   to be a resident world. The first "it was there all along."
2. **`WH`: one count, four costumes** — roots of `t² = p` = rails =
   eigenvalues = orbit shape. The June-24 spine; the proto-summit. Seam B×C.
3. **`CN`: the knife is the dial** — `p = a² − 1`; conic sections and number
   planes are one classification. The terrace steps; seam B×C, walkable in
   the app today (the cone view).
4. **`QD`: the story in miniature** — `t² = p` is itself a quadratic;
   discriminant plays p. Seam A×C, and the gate to `FTA`.
5. **`PT`: the family self-describes** — d/dp at p = 0 is the dual
   direction; differentiate the family of planes at its middle and it
   answers in dual numbers. Seam C×B (the middle parterre appears *inside*
   the parameter space).
6. **`NH`: the sticky middle, at every scale** — planes, conics, curvature,
   fixed points: two open classes pressed against a measure-zero wall.
   The seam that repeats fractally; `CK`'s Galilean center is doubly sticky.
7. **`IN`: the master seam** — every trichotomy in the garden is Sylvester's
   signature in costume. The summit where all three paths are visible as one.
8. **`CR`: the loop closure** — `x ↔ y` is `p ↦ 1/p`; dual at both 0 and ∞;
   the three planes are one circle, not three boxes. Seam within C that bends
   the plaza's "line of choices" closed.
9. **`CK`'s rhyme** *(flagged honestly as rhyme, not theorem)* — the moduli
   picture of the nine geometries (4 quadrants + 4 rays + origin) has the
   anatomy of the split plane's own portrait. A decorative seam; label it as
   such.

And one **meta-seam**, worth a marginal note in the notebook itself: the
project's own history walks Path C. June 24's "one idea, four costumes"
(`WH`) grew into June 29's "one theorem in costume" (`IN`). The garden was
designed by walking it.

## Garden principles for the presentation

What the analogy commits the notebook design to (the balance of goals in
Dan's brief, made operational):

1. **The visitor walks paths; plantings are read from the path.** A thread
   (path) is the reading surface; a card is a planting seen from it. Depth is
   *proximity*, not navigation: `glance` from the path, `note` when you stop,
   `full` when you kneel at the bed. No "click through to read more" page
   jumps — leaning in happens in place.
2. **Junctions are places.** Where two paths share a card (the nine seams),
   the notebook shows the crossing: you can see the other path's direction
   from here, and switching is one step. This is "many paths, natural to the
   layout" — choice exists exactly at junctions, not everywhere.
3. **Gates are honest.** Orbs render as gates: visible through (the glance),
   clearly marked "not yet in the book." Curated means bounded.
4. **The walk step is the rhythm.** Every stop = question → reframe →
   instrument. A stop's closing question is the next stop's opening — that is
   the thread. Cards mostly already have this shape; the thread layer adds
   only the connective questions.
5. **Instruments are planted in their beds.** App views and widgets embed
   where the card lives (the card `figures:` field already points the right
   way. The app is not a separate toolshed the notebook links out to; the
   relevant view appears at the stop that needs it.
6. **The graph is the gardener's plan, not the visitor's map.** The
   inspector (list/graph views) stays a working tool; visitors get paths and
   junctions, never the hairball.
