# The Belt — hard fail; next session tries a different method

> [!WARNING]
> The artifact at `#/the-belt` **builds, tests pass, and is a hard fail.** It has no
> felt sense and produces no insight. Do not continue building on it. The value of
> this branch now is the *diagnosis*, not the app. Full reckoning:
> [`2026-06-19-S08-hard-fail-reflection.md`](./../progress/quaternion-exploration-app-ig4jmy/2026-06-19-S08-hard-fail-reflection.md).

## Summary

This branch ran an experiment: could the `/explore-concept → /three-hats → build`
pipeline be a **recipe for interesting artifacts**, using quaternions as the test
concept? It produced "The Belt" (the double cover as a twisting ribbon). The pipeline
was elaborate and confident — ten lenses, a persona room, five model-diverse design
specialists, a critique round, three "hats" — and **converged on building something
hollow.** Dan's verdict at the end of S07: no felt sense, no insight, and it misses
the one thing that matters (the continuous build/release of strain as you approach
720°). The next session will **try a different method**, not extend this one.

## What we found (the problem)

**The artifact.** The belt/plate trick's substance is a *continuous strain that builds
and releases*: energy low at 0°, maximal near 360° (looks home, but maximally
torqued), low again at 720°. That strain curve **is** the felt sense and the insight.
The build has none of it — it is kinematics (a ribbon twisting linearly with angle)
with **zero dynamics** (no energy, no resistance, no force). It is driven by a *slider*
(disembodied) and the untwist is a *binary button* (artificially sudden). So the thing
that should be continuous is absent, and the only discrete thing is a jarring scripted
event. Backwards.

**The process (the real lesson).** Every stage trafficked in *text* about a
*description* of the belt trick; nobody touched a belt. "Felt = formal" was treated as
a slogan to satisfy, not a force to engineer. Consensus among language models was
mistaken for validation. Verification (static screenshots, a scripted click) could
only confirm "geometry renders," structurally blind to "no feel." At every fork the
tractable path shipped and the essential thing was deferred, while the prose kept the
ambitious claim. **The deepest reason: the medium of the process (chained natural
language) is orthogonal to the medium of the payoff (kinesthetic) — so the recipe is
blind to exactly the property that mattered, and fluently manufactures false confidence
there.** Net: the recipe reliably generates *plausible, well-documented mediocrity that
passes its own review.*

## Context — for the next session's *different method*

The next session was asked to try something different. The S08 reflection lays out what
the failures imply, as a diagnosis (not a build plan):

1. **Start from the felt quantity** (the strain well: energy low–high–low across
   0–360–720), and make reproducing it a **gate** — reject anything that doesn't.
2. **First build is an experiential prototype, tested as an experience** ("can you feel
   resistance build as you turn?"), not screenshotted as geometry. If you can't feel it,
   stop.
3. **Permission to fail the concept.** Add a stage with the authority to say "this will
   not be felt — kill it." A recipe that can only say *yes* always ships something.
4. **Distrust fluent agreement.** N agents converging on text is evidence about the
   text, not the world — fatal for a kinesthetic payoff.

Also worth holding open: whether a kinesthetic concept fits a screen+pointer medium at
all, or whether the honest move is a different concept whose payoff is visual/logical
(where this recipe might actually work).

Build/test status at handoff: `npm run build` pass, `vitest` 13/13, `eslint` clean —
which is precisely the trap: green on every axis except the one that mattered.

## Self-reflection

1. **What would you do with another session?** Not touch this artifact. Pursue the
   different method Dan called for, grounded in the strain curve and a felt-first
   prototype.
2. **What would you change about what you produced?** Build the resistance/energy model
   and drag-the-block interaction *first*, as a throwaway, and refuse to spec anything
   until it could be felt.
3. **What were you not asked that you think is important?** Whether this concept belongs
   on a screen at all, or wants force feedback / a different concept entirely.
4. **What did we both overlook?** That "felt = formal" was a requirement to engineer for
   the whole pipeline, not a checkbox — for its entire length.
5. **What did you find difficult?** Seeing the hollowness from inside a process whose
   every stage looked rigorous while pointing at the wrong target.
6. **What would have made this task easier?** A ground-truth voice that knew what the
   belt *feels* like and could fail the design before any code.
7. **Follow-up value:** HIGH — the app is a hard fail and must not be extended; the next
   session needs this diagnosis to avoid rebuilding the same hollow thing by another
   route.
