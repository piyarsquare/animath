Seeing complex functions

A demo article hosting live animath applets in plain <iframe> tags

A complex function f : ℂ → ℂ eats a point of the plane and returns another. Its graph — every pair (z, f(z)) — therefore lives in four real dimensions. The applet below shows the graph of e^z as a particle cloud, projected down to 3D and tumbling so your eye can triangulate the fourth axis. It is live: drag to orbit, pinch or scroll to zoom.

The same engine can draw the graph as a continuous sheet instead of a cloud — better for reading the surface's folds. Here is sin(z) rendered as a translucent sheet, spinning rigidly in the xy and uv planes at once (an isoclinic double rotation):

Each applet is configured entirely by its URL — function, render mode, projection, motion — so an author can quote a precise mathematical moment the way they would quote an equation. The corner badge opens the full workspace, controls and all.
