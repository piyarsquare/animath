Step to two coordinates. Addition rides along unchanged. Multiplication — under the same demands — collapses to a single number: what j² equals.

SPIN

p < 0
  • Multiplying turns the plane.
  • No direction holds its line — zero rails.
  • Every nonzero number divides.
j² negative — elliptic

SHEAR

p = 0
  • Multiplying slides — one held line.
  • One rail, repeated.
  • The slide carries the derivative.
j² = 0 — parabolic, degenerate

BOOST

p > 0
  • Multiplying squeezes along a hyperbola.
  • Two rails hold their lines.
  • The strangers’ multiply, in disguise.
j² positive — hyperbolic

Alternate derivation

Let z = x + jy, with j² = p. Pair it with its conjugate z̄ = x − jy:  z·z̄ = x² − p·y²  ≡  N(z).
N(zw) = N(z)·N(w) — the invariant every plane respects. Its level sets are the geometry above; its null set is where division dies.
