## note

Turn the dial and only the **sign** of `p` matters. Three cases, with `−1`, `0`,
`+1` as the clean representatives:

- `p < 0` → complex [[CX]]
- `p = 0` → dual [[DU]]
- `p > 0` → split [[SP]]

## full

Rescaling `j` sends any `p` to `−1`, `0`, or `+1`, so up to isomorphism there are
exactly three commutative unital planes. The sign of `p` is the invariant; the three
are genuinely different rings. [[WH]]
