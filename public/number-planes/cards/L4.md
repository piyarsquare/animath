## note

Give a number one coordinate and apply the demands. They force the whole table —
even `(−1)·(−1) = +1`. That sign rule is a theorem, not a convention.

The line leaves **no** choices. (The plane will leave exactly one.)

## full

Every integer is a sum of `±1`s, so distributivity over the additive group computes
every product; `(−1)(−1) = +1` falls out of `0 = (−1)(1 + (−1))`. On `ℚ`, then `ℝ`
(with continuity), the product is the unique one. The only escape is to drop the
additive group — to give up subtraction. [[tropical]]
