## note

**Sylvester's law of inertia:** a quadratic form, up to change of basis and
scale, is completely described by a finite list of signs — its signature.

Every classification this notebook has met is that theorem in costume:

- **2 variables**: the signature of `x² − p·y²` → the three number planes
  (sticky dual between them).
- **3 variables, the null cone, sliced** → the conics (sticky parabola).
- **3 variables, the nonzero levels** → the curved worlds themselves: spheres
  (`κ > 0`), two-sheeted hyperboloids (the hyperbolic plane — the hyperboloid
  model), one-sheeted hyperboloids (de Sitter) — with **the cone as the sticky
  middle of the family of worlds**.

`j² = p` was a one-variable signature choice all along.

## full

On a curved world the roles of the knife reverse: flat-row conics came from
*offset* slices of the cone; the **geodesics** of a curved world are its
*central* slices — great circles on the sphere, hyperbolic lines on the
hyperboloid. Same knife, new job.

Signs are why everything came in threes with a fragile middle: a finite list of
signs cannot vary continuously, so every family classified by signature has
open generic classes pressed against sticky degenerate walls ([[NH]]). The
4-quadrants-4-rays-origin picture ([[CK]]) is the atlas of these sign choices
for the plane geometries; this card is the reason the atlas looks the way it
does.
