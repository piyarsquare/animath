## note

`t² = p` is the whole story in miniature: two roots, one, or none, as `p` is
positive, zero, negative. The rail count, the plane, and "how many solutions does
this quadratic have" are one question. [[FTA]]

## full

A quadratic's discriminant plays `p`'s role: its sign sets the number of real roots
(2 / 1 / 0). Forcing roots to always exist is forcing `√(negative)` to live
somewhere — the complex plane, and the doorway to the fundamental theorem of
algebra. [[FTA]]
