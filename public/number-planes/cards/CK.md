## note

*Open tab — not yet in the book.* Klein: a geometry is a space plus its motions.
Cayley: fix one conic (the "absolute") and derive all measurement from it. Two
dials — **angle** (our `p`) and **distance/curvature** (`κ`) — each a rescalable
invariant, so only signs survive.

**The right picture is a plane cut by a coordinate cross**: `p` across, `κ` up.
Four open quadrants (hyperbolic · elliptic · anti-de Sitter · de Sitter), four
rays (Euclidean · Minkowski on the `κ = 0` axis; the two curved-Galilean
Newton–Hooke geometries on the `p = 0` axis), one origin (Galilean).
4 + 4 + 1 = 9. Closure = adjacency: each ray is the sticky wall of its two
quadrants; the origin is sticky for all nine. [[NH]]

The number planes are the horizontal axis (`κ = 0`); the dial in the app walks
it. And note the rhyme (a rhyme, not a theorem): quadrants, cross, special
origin — the moduli picture of the nine geometries has the anatomy of the split
plane's own portrait. (Yaglom's *A Simple Non-Euclidean Geometry* lives at the
origin.) → expand later.
