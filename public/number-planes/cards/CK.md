## note

*Open tab — not yet in the book.* Klein: a geometry is a space plus its motions.
Cayley: fix one conic (the "absolute") and derive all measurement from it. In
the plane the choice splits into two dials — how you measure **distance** and
how you measure **angle**, each elliptic · parabolic · hyperbolic — a 3×3 table
of nine geometries. The number planes are the **ordinary-distance row**:
Euclidean · Galilean · Minkowski, whose angle measures are exactly spin · shear
· boost. The second dial (curvature) climbs the columns — a whole axis we
haven't turned. (Yaglom's *A Simple Non-Euclidean Geometry* lives in the middle
cell.) → expand later.
