## note

Addition is fixed ([[AX]]). Multiplication is not.

There's more than one way to multiply two points. The plainest is coordinate by
coordinate:

`(a,b) · (c,d) = (a·c, b·d)`

It works. It just isn't complex. So what do we demand of `×` that picks the complex
one out? ([[L3]])

That rival isn't a strawman — it's `ℝ×ℝ`, a real number system we meet again as the
split plane. [[SP]]

## full

Ask only that `×` be bilinear — distribute over `+` from both sides — and carry a
unit `1`. On the plane that still leaves a whole *family* of products, not one: the
coordinate-wise rule above is one member, the complex rule another, the dual rule a
third. Pinning down *the* complex product takes a further demand ([[FD]], [[L5]]);
the family it lives in, and the one knob that indexes it, are [[PL]].
