## note

Try the simplest thing: treat the two coordinates as strangers — multiply each on
its own.

`(a,b) · (c,d) = (a·c, b·d)`

A real, working multiplication. Follow where it leads and it turns out to be one
particular plane: `j² = 1`, the split one. [[SP]]

## full

Coordinate-wise product on `ℝ²` is the ring `ℝ × ℝ` — two independent number lines.
In the basis `e± = (1 ± j)/2` it reads `j² = 1`: idempotents `e±² = e±`, `e₊e₋ = 0`,
zero divisors and all. "The slots are strangers" *is* the split plane in disguise.
[[SP]]
