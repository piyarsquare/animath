The math and the code behind the complex-function viewers — each page pairs a live applet with the exact source lines that compute it

These six pages take the Complex Particles and Plane Transform viewers apart and explain how they work, from the functions through the projections to the pixels. They read like a code manual crossed with a short math course: drag the embedded applets, then read the routine that drew them. Start anywhere — they cross-link.

The math — from a function to your screen

  1. What the functions do
    How each complex function is computed, and what that does to the plane it transforms — angle-doubling, poles, branches, and the functions that are secretly ez.
    src/lib/complexMath.ts
  2. From 4D to your screen
    The 4-D graph of (z, f(z)), the projections that flatten it (Perspective, Drop-axis, Torus, the Hopf sphere), and the quaternion rotations that turn it.
    src/lib/viewpoint.ts · src/math/quat4.ts
  3. How the surface is drawn
    The render modes — Points, Sheet, Tiles, Net — and adaptive density: how a cloud of samples becomes a picture, and how it crowds toward where the function works hardest.
    src/lib/particles/createParticleGeometry.ts
